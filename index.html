Vittorio Jano

The man who brought success to Alfa Romeo

Here is a timeline of Vittorio Jano's life:

  • Born in San Girogio Canavese as Viktor Janos to Hungarian migrants
  • 1911 He started to work for FIAT under Luigi Bazzi
  • 1923 Replace Giuseppe Merosi as chief engineer at Alfa Romeo
  • 1925 His first design was the 8-cylinder in-line mounted P2 Grand Prix car, which won Alfa Romeo the inaugural world championship for Grand Prix cars
  • 1932 He produced the sensational P3 model which later was raced with great success by Enzo Ferrari
  • 1936 He designed the Alfa Romeo 12C using a V12 engine
  • 1937 He left Alfa Romeo and moved to Lancia
  • 1954 He designs the Lancia D50 with a V8 engine
  • 1956 He is moving to Scuderia Ferrari and designs the Jano V12 engine for the Ferrari 290MM with succes of 2x World Championship win
  • 1959 With Enzo Ferraris's son Alfredo "Dino" Ferrari they create the Dino V6 engine
  • 1965 Like Enzo Ferrari, Jano lost his own son. He became gravely ill that same year and committed suicide in Turin

“Race cars are neither beautiful nor ugly. They become beautiful when they win.” Enzo Ferrari
